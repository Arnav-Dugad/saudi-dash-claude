Remarkable Facts About Saudi Arabia

Did You Know?
World's Largest Oil Exporter — Saudi Arabia controls nearly 17% of proven global oil reserves and is the world's largest oil exporter, shipping over 7 million barrels daily to international markets.
Mecca — Islam's Holiest City — The Grand Mosque in Mecca can hold up to 4 million worshippers at a time. Over 1.8 billion Muslims worldwide face Mecca to pray 5 times daily.
No Rivers in Saudi Arabia — Saudi Arabia is one of the few countries with no permanent rivers or lakes. It relies on desalination plants producing 7.4 million m³/day — the world's largest capacity.
Arabian Camel Capital — Saudi Arabia has the world's largest camel population at 8.8 million. The annual King Abdulaziz Camel Festival awards over SAR 300M in prizes.
Extreme Temperatures — Saudi Arabia experiences some of Earth's most extreme heat. The Empty Quarter (Rub' al Khali) desert is the world's largest sand desert at 650,000 km².
Falconry Heritage — Saudi Arabia is home to some of the world's most expensive falcons, with a single Peregrine selling for up to SAR 1.5M. UNESCO lists Saudi falconry as Intangible Cultural Heritage.
Zero Income Tax — Saudi nationals pay zero income tax. The government provides free healthcare, education, and heavily subsidized utilities to citizens funded by oil revenues.
Youngest Governance — Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman is one of the world's most powerful leaders under 40. He oversees the transformation of a nation of $3 trillion in economic plans.
Sun Year-Round — Saudi Arabia receives 3,000+ hours of sunshine annually — making it a solar energy goldmine. Plans to build a 200 GW solar capacity by 2030 are underway.
