Remarkable Facts About the Kingdom

Did You Know?
World's Largest Oil Exporter — Controls 17% of proven global reserves. Aramco exports over 7M barrels daily. At current production, Saudi reserves will last 70+ years.
Mecca — 1.8 Billion Face Daily — The Grand Mosque holds 4 million worshippers simultaneously. Every Muslim on Earth faces Mecca to pray 5 times daily — a constant connection to the Kingdom.
No Natural Rivers — World's Largest Desalination — Saudi Arabia has no permanent rivers. It runs 35 major desalination plants producing 7.4M m³/day — more than any nation on Earth.
8.8M Camel Capital of the World — Saudi Arabia hosts the world's largest camel population. The King Abdulaziz Camel Festival distributes over SAR 300M in prizes annually — and uses AI to judge camel beauty.
The Empty Quarter — World's Largest Sand Desert — Rub' al Khali covers 650,000 km², larger than France. Temperatures exceed 56°C in summer. It holds vast undiscovered oil deposits beneath its dunes.
Falconry — UNESCO Heritage — A single prized Saker falcon can sell for SAR 1.5M+. Saudi falconry spans 2,000 years and is listed as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Falcons get their own passports.
Zero Income Tax for Saudi Citizens — Nationals pay no income tax. The state provides free education, free healthcare, subsidized fuel, water & electricity — all funded by petrodollars.
3,000+ Hours of Sunshine Annually — Saudi Arabia is a solar goldmine with plans for 200 GW solar capacity by 2030. The NEOM Green Hydrogen project will be the world's largest, producing 600 tonnes/day.
MBS — Youngest Major World Leader — Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman oversees plans worth over $3 trillion. Under his leadership, women gained driving rights (2018), cinemas reopened after 35 years, and Vision 2030 launched.
THE LINE — Engineering Miracle — NEOM's THE LINE will stand 500 metres tall, stretch 170km long, but only 200 metres wide. AI manages everything. No cars, no roads, no carbon emissions. All residents within 5 min walk of any amenity.
Qahwa — Arabian Coffee Culture — Cardamom-spiced Saudi coffee (Qahwa) is a UNESCO-listed cultural tradition. Refusing coffee when offered is considered impolite. Saudi Arabia consumes 33,000 tonnes of coffee annually.
Al-Ula — 200,000-Year-Old History — Al-Ula's Hegra (Madain Salih) is Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site — 111 Nabataean tombs carved into rose-red rock formations, predating Petra in Jordan by centuries.
